BA or BS in Climate Change Studies

CU Denver sits at the edge of the Rocky Mountain West — a region where wildfire seasons stretch longer every year, water supplies are contested, and the policy decisions that shape the next century are being made right now. This is the backdrop for your education.

The Climate Change Studies program brings together natural science, policy, and social justice in a curriculum built for students who want more than a diploma — they want to do something with it. Three specialized tracks let you go deep in the direction that fits you, with room to double major, minor, or certificate across disciplines.

You'll learn from faculty who are active researchers, work alongside community organizations in Denver, and graduate ready for careers in environmental policy, advocacy, research, and sustainability practice.

Program features

Our program is one of a small number in the country that treats climate change as a genuinely interdisciplinary subject — not just a science problem, or a policy problem, but both at once. You choose your track, but you graduate with fluency across all three.

CU Denver's location gives you access to Colorado's network of environmental agencies, nonprofits, and research institutions. Students have worked with Denver's Office of Climate Action, Colorado's Department of Natural Resources, and community organizations along the Front Range.

Three specialized tracks

Natural Sciences (BS), Climate & Policy (BA), or Climate & Society (BA) — tailor your degree to your goals.

Field and lab work

Hands-on research embedded in the curriculum, not an add-on.

Denver as your classroom

Real partnerships with local agencies, NGOs, and the Front Range policy community.

4+1 pathway available

Complete your BA/BS and a master's degree in five years through connected graduate programs.
